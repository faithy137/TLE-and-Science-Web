What is Magnesium?

Magnesium (Mg), chemical element, one of the alkaline-earth metals of Group 2 (IIa) of the periodic table, and the lightest structural metal. Its compounds are widely used in construction and medicine, and magnesium is one of the elements essential to all cellular life.

Why is it Important?
  1. Involved in hundreds of biochemical reactions in your body
    2. Magnesium is essential for your body's function, present in every cell and involved in over 600 processes. It helps convert food into energy, create proteins, maintain DNA and RNA, support muscle movements, and regulate the nervous system by managing neurotransmitters.
  2. May boost exercise performance
    3. Magnesium supplements have been shown to enhance exercise performance in several studies, but results are mixed.
  3. May combat depression
    4. Magnesium deficiency may be linked to depression. As such, supplementing may help reduce symptoms of depression.
  4. May support healthy blood sugar levels
    5. Magnesium supplements may improve blood sugar levels, insulin sensitivity, and other risk factors for type 2 diabetes.
  5. May promote heart health
    6. Magnesium may help lower blood pressure levels and reduce several risk factors for heart disease. Still, more research is needed. Where can we find Magnesium? Magnesium is essential for many aspects of health. The recommended daily intake is 400–420 mg for males and 310–320 mg for females.
    You can get this mineral from both food and supplements.
    Food sources:

    Supplements

    If you have a medical condition, check with your doctor before taking magnesium supplements. Though these supplements are generally well tolerated, they may be unsafe for people who take certain diuretics, heart medications, osteoporosis medications, proton pump inhibitors, or antibiotics.
    Forms that tend to be absorbed well include magnesium citrate, glycinate, orotate, and carbonate.

    How do we use Magnesium?

    Magnesium is required for energy production, oxidative phosphorylation, and glycolysis. It contributes to the structural development of bone and is required for the synthesis of DNA, RNA, and the antioxidant glutathione.
    Magnesium supplements should be taken with meals. Taking magnesium supplements on an empty stomach may cause diarrhea.
